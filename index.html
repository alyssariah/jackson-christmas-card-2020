ALYSSA & BRIAN Recap of our favorite moments from 2020
Join our adventure

January

We made the best of President’s Day weekend and camped out in Redwood National Park, camping right along the river bank. One of our less-organized outings, we forgot some of the basics like matches and utensils, but overall it was a great trip!

February 20

Right before Alyssa started her coding bootcamp, Alyssa’s parents and brother flew out to California to spend some time with us. Our favorite outing was visiting Alcatraz. So glad they visited it before we moved!

February 29

Brian didn’t get in as much as hiking this year as he’d prefer, but was able to get away with our friend Craig to “scratch the itch,” scratching the 20-mile Sea-to-Summit Cone Peak hike off the list. It was one of Brian’s favorite hikes so far!

March-April

Right as the California shelter-in-place restrictions were tightening down, we decided to foster a husky. Morgan was a crazy, stubborn, and sassy pup who loves to run, chew, and chase everything. Although working from home in our 300 sqft studio with a husky had its challenges, we have so many good memories! After spending 2 months with us, Morgan found a good home.

May 16

Before we left California, we had to do one last drive up and down the PCH. Alyssa’s Aunt Geri (and Uncle Troy) was very generous and let us borrow her BMW Z3 Roadster for the day, so we can now say we’ve driven the PCH in a convertible. And yes, that turkey-cheddar-bacon sandwich on fresh-baked SF sourdough was as good as it looks!

June 15

Definitely one of the highlights of the year, we got to visit Yosemite a week after it re-opened, and thanks to COVID, enjoyed it without the crowds. Not sure how, but Alyssa agreed to spend another anniversary backpacking in a tent! The hike up to the North Rim was gruelling, but the views from our campsite made it worth it!

June-July

Road trippin’! As part of our move out East, we made it closer to our goal of visiting all of the National Parks, checking off Yosemite, Great Basin, Bryce Canyon, Mesa Verde, and Black Canyon of the Gunnison. We got in a lot of great hikes (and others where Alyssa hated Brian afterwards).

July-August

We got to Pittsburgh! It’s grown so much on both of us already. We love the green hills, beautiful rivers, breathtaking fall colors, and many parks. The people here have been incredibly kind, welcoming, and friendly (sorry Texas, Pittsburgh has you beat). Although the pandemic has made it difficult to get to know people, we’re excited to make new friends and get to know the city even better over the next several years.

September 15

We closed on our first home! The process was long and difficult, but after nearly 4 months of living out of a car and suitcases, we were ready to have a place of our own. It’s a 100 years old and has some quirks, but we love it! We have a stream going through our backyard, are a 2 minute walk from the Monongahela River trail, and a 5 mile bike ride to CMU (which Brian may end up doing as some point, but for now is taking classes and doing research from home).

October-November

Construction project time! We tackled the pretty ambitious task of building a 30 ft suspension bridge across the creek in our backyard. Brian dusted off his old engineering textbooks, and hauled a bunch of cement. Brian’s brother Hyrum came out to help before being stationed overseas. It took a while, and still has more to do, but at least we have access to our backyard now!

October 25

Alyssa got her puppy! For those of you who don’t know, saying that Alyssa loves dogs is an understatement. In California she volunteered with Guide Dogs for the Blind and worked as a trainer at the Humane Society and Petco. She’s been patiently waiting for her own dog for a long time! River is a fiesty Golden Irish who loves attention but is stubbornly independent. She’s been a challenge but Alyssa is up for it!
